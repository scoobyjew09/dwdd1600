Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship

Digital citizenship is the responsible, ethical, and safe use of technology and online spaces, involving respectful behavior, digital literacy, awareness of one's online footprint, and understanding rights and responsibilities in the digital world

What is Digital Law

Digital law (or cyber law) is the body of legal rules and principles governing activities, rights, and responsibilities in the digital world, regulating online behavior, data, technology use, and electronic transactions to address challenges like privacy, security, intellectual property, and cybercrime.

What is AUP?

AUP most commonly stands for Acceptable Use Policy, a set of rules defining permitted and prohibited uses of an organization's network, devices, and data, ensuring security, compliance, and responsible behavior.

What is Plagiarism?

Plagiarism is presenting someone else's words, ideas, or work as your own without giving proper credit, essentially stealing intellectual property and committing fraud, which can happen intentionally or accidentally.

What is Academic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty is any act that gives a student an unfair advantage in learning or assessment, violating ethical standards and institutional policies, and includes behaviors like plagiarism, cheating, fabrication, collusion (unauthorized group work), and sabotage, undermining the integrity of education.

It's basically cheating or lying in school related works.

What are the rules for AI Generated Content?

Rules for AI-generated content focus on human accountability, transparency (disclosure), copyright issues (AI content isn't typically copyrightable), and avoiding harmful/illegal uses
